Discussion of design

Our design achieves a high standard of coupling due to successfuly encaspulating data in very specific entity classes, and only allowing the funneling of their data through control classes. We have deliberately designed our functions to never interact with data that is not relevant to their scope. Entities never directly interact with other entities, and are only accessed or modified by their respective controller. For cohesion, we have successfuly deduced the appropriate number and roles of the different classes necessary for our software's operation, and grouped attributes and methods to these classes appropriately. We have no cross-over or bloat wherein a class consumes multiple different roles; instead, we divvy up data and function according to what that class has been specifically designed to do. Thus, our design has coupled solely by data, and our cohesion is extremely functional. Therefore our design will not encounter faults due to inappropriately handled data, or have redundant complexity due to bloated classes, which is the essence of good design.
